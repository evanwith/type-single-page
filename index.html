Extendomatic

Designed for flexibility

Extendomatic is a variable font that adapts to different design needs, reminiscent of old signage from the 1950's. It can be curvy and cute or geometric and rigid.

Variable Font

History of the font

Created May 1st of 2021, David Jonathan Ross designed Extendomatic by focusing on the skeleton of the font. The decorative display font is available in almost forty languages, including Dutch, Vietnamese, Zulu, and many more. Ross was inspired by old signage on buildings in LA. While initially, Ross wanted to follow a retro theme, he strayed away from that to focus more on the geometry of the font. By messing with things like weight, width, tracking, and more, Ross has made the font more versatile, where it can lean into that geometric feel or the more retro feel, based on whatever the project requires.

Supported Languages include:
